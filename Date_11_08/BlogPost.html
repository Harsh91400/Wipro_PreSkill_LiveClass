The Mahabharata is one of the greatest ancient epics of India,
written by Maharishi Ved Vyasa. It tells the story of two families,
the Pandavas and the Kauravas, who fought a major war known as the Battle
of Kurukshetra. The epic explores themes of duty, justice, loyalty, and
the consequences of human actions. A very important part of the Mahabharata
is the Bhagavad Gita, where Lord Krishna guides Arjuna on the meaning of life
and righteous action. The Mahabharata is not just a story of war but a source
of moral teachings and practical wisdom that still guide people today.
